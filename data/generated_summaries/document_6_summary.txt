This document summarizes a meeting focused on the project kickoff and requirements review for the project, including a detailed agenda, discussion points, and action items.

**Key Findings:**

*   The program overview and objectives were clearly defined.
*   The radiation hardening satellite ADC development program (RHADC) is a critical part of the project.
*   The requirement for a 16-bit, 250 MSPS analog-to-digital converter for deployment in low Earth orbit (LEO) and medium Earth orbit (MEO) satellite payloads was met.
*   The CQFP-80 ceramic package was selected as the baseline for the analog design.
*   The EGR-3 layout kit was mandatory for all analog blocks.
*   A preliminary power feasibility analysis was conducted for the 16-bit 250 MSPS ADC on the GS180H.
*   The project schedule is described as aggressive, with a high level of reliability qualification.
*   The resource allocation is expected to be sufficient, with a focus on the 180 nm rad-hard process.
*   The risk of power consumption exceeding 450 mW is a key concern.

*   The project is scheduled for the next meeting, 25 February 2025.

**Risks Identified:**

*   Power consumption exceeding 450 mW due to RHBD overhead on the 16-bit 250 MSPS architecture.
*   The EGR-3 layout kit availability and cost are significant challenges.
*   The power feasibility study is likely to be inadequate before the architecture review.
*   The schedule is aggressive, potentially leading to a delay in the final product.
*   The lack of sufficient radiation test facilities for the TID and SEE testing is a potential risk.
*   The availability of external radiation test facilities might cause schedule slippage.
